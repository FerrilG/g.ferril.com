Overview

Problem

I worked as a consultant alongside two other UX designers to enhance the client’s internal time tracker. The project focus was to identify user pain points and potential solutions that would save time, address accuracy, and encourage consistent submission of their timesheets.

Challenge

Designing a solution at an enterprise level requires insight into all facets that drive the product. This level of attention involves administrative and end-user involvement to understand the technical constraints and business requirements of the project.

Solution

We introduced new features that would assist users logging their development time more accurately and reduce the mundane repetitive tasks frequently requested by administration.

Research

Discovery

In order to design with a user centric mindset, we decided to include the users in a few of our design sessions because of our direct access to them. This allowed us to have a constant contact with them to assist with any questions we had.

Design Workshop

We brought users in to our design workshops so they could sketch and design their ideas. We then asked them to elaborate on their designs and listened to their responses.

By including the users, we were able to observe and outline their individual concerns through their mockups. The team was able to take our initial findings back and outline major pain points.

Concept Iteration

Taking our initial findings, we went to the drawing board and started thinking about holistic approaches to the problem. This allowed us to think about pie in the sky concepts and then reel them back to meet constraints.

Initial Concepts

I sketched a few rough concepts with the team to annotate the features and functionality.

Usability Testing

I then built the new features into a robust prototype to demonstrate functionality to users. We brought this back to the client’s location to conduct usability tests to identify areas for improvement.

Synthesis

We took all our findings and created a comprehensive list that outlined our key insights. We used this to finalize our concept and proceed to client handoff.

Research findings
Undefined definition of project, bucket, and their relationship

Projects are defined as individual tasks assigned to teams. A bucket is a task in which employees are assigned and they log their time to.

Many people have these terms confused by thinking the Bucket is the “Parent” of the Projects. Buckets include Admin, Inter-sourcing, Support, Enhancements, and Projects.

Bucket Names:
  • Admin has a collection of tasks that are relative to non-productive time. This bucket includes tasks such as: PTO, meetings, and training etc.
  • Inter-Sourcing is cross-departmental work.
  • Enhancement is also perpetual but is tied to specific products.
  • Project includes tasks defined as a temporary endeavor done to produce something as a goal or end product.
Note: Having a Bucket named “Projects” added to the confusion.
Limited Search Functionality

Users explained their issues when findings the projects they were supposed to track their time to. If they were unable to find the proper location, their time would be allocated improperly. This issue is due to the current naming system. By adding a project description, set by the manager, users would be able to search for terms or keywords in the description.

Lack of Automation / Project Import

Users want to login, track their time, and log out. Often, they do this while referencing another calendar, schedule, or time tracking tool. As they expressed how it feels like a redundant task, users indicated they wanted a way to automatically populate the projects they work on the most frequent. This would reduce most of the repetition and allow them to log time easier.

Final solution

Main page

This is the main page of the application. From here users are able to select and search from the list of available projects on the left. Users found the labels unidentifiable, so we introduced the concept of a description area below the label.

Presets

Users found it annoying when they had to add items repetitively. We added a template/preset section to auto-populate their dashboard with their self-defined projects. This allowed them to focus on inputting their time and mitigate time spent searching.

Optional replace

To prevent issues and offer flexibility, users liked having the ability to add presets to their current selection. This way users can add items prior to using their presets.
