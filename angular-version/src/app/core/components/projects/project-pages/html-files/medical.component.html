Overview

Problem

BackpackEMR required a metrics module to be developed to allow volunteers, administration, and financial supporters to gauge the operational efficiency during and after medical mission trips.

Challenge

The primary challenge I had was the lack of direct access to the users. The client's users were rather specific, and any type of communication required the client to be transparent with their customers.

Solution

I identified the minimum viable experience (MVE) required that would allow users to evaluate the operational efficiency of their mission trip. This included patientâ€™s condition and status, medical inventory tracking, and exportable trip metrics.

Research

At this point, I had to generalize different types of users and their pain points based on my initial ramp-up. I created personas for each of the users and combined the overlap of goals into a journey map. I then used it as a map to highlight the areas that needed the most help for the concepts.

Concepts

I chose to utilize a kano approach to try and get the most information from the users due to limited access. I created multiple low fidelity mockups of features to share with users to have them respond with their feedback and ratings. This allowed me to get insight into how they would expect these features to function.

Survey findings

I received the feedback from users and then compared their ratings of the various features. I used these ratings to drive which items I should focus on. I then added definition to the designs and had development provide rough estimates.

Final solution

Patient Progress

Development Time: 6 hours
This module provides the staff with the ability to modify patient progress and update their status.

Daily Metrics

Development Time: 24 hours
Allows the administration to view metrics about the patients seen, bottlenecks and surgeries performed. This would allow them to manage inventory better.

Annual Metrics

Development Time: 24 hours
Allows the administration to compare their efforts at a year-to-date or previous year level. This will allow staff to accurately plan future campaigns.
