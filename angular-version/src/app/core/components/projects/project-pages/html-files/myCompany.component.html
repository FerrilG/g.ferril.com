Overview

The internal sales team found it difficult to sell their eCommerce product. The assumption was that it didn't have features the client wanted for their customers.

I compared user pain points with a heuristic evaluation and supportive research to support a sitewide redesign that would address user concerns and meet stakeholder requirements.

During the middle of the project an escalation regarding the current platform and ADA compliance required our project decisions to pivot.

Approach

Budget and scope of this project was limited and didn't allow many opportunities for feature enhancements. Therfore the design team needed to be methodical with our approach to ensure enhancements were strategic.

  1. Unpack Project
  2. Concept solution
  3. Iterate
  4. Phased delivery
Success criteria

We bagan by unpacking the business needs and what spelled success for them:

  • Remove all ADA WCAG 2.0(AA) defects
  • Create a modern site for end-users
  • Provide a way for users to save items

We then needed to verify if the initial business goals were inline with the users' needs. I then walked through and spoke with end-users and clients to understand their thoughts. Their response indicated a different set of needs not provided by the system.

Expected functions were not present and prevented the flexibility of the eCommerce service:

  • Unable to easily find items
  • Point value was hard to identify
  • Browsing methods were difficult and not flexible

Design Concepts

Display of items

Modification to the cart began with making the images larger. After that bringing displaying the the selected options and a larger price was introduced as part of assisting user visibility. From here we incorporated a new feature to allow users to assign the configuration to a wishlist. This would allow the user to preload the options and skip the selection process.

Development

For this project we were working with a remote team of developers who had limited front-end experience. This presented additional challenges late in the project and we needed to adapt. Since we were not in the same place, timezone (developers were on the east coast), and limited experience; my deliverables needed to be presented in a well laid out manner. In addition to my presentation we utilized Zeplin to assist the styling so our handoff could focus on functionality.
